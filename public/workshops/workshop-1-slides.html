Mindset

The Five Golden Rules

🎯
Be Precise
One task at a time. Break down complex features into smaller, unambiguous instructions.
🧱
Be Organised
Add features step-by-step. Test after each addition and roll back if something breaks.
🔄
Start Fresh
Use a new chat or session for each major feature. This makes it easier to track changes and debug.
⏳
Be Patient
Read the AI's explanations to learn as you go. Debugging is 70% of the process, even for pros.
✅
Review & Trust
Question things that don't make sense, but also trust that an unexpected AI solution might be creative and effective.

Before You Build

Break It Down First

Close the loop before you start — so you don't discover missing pieces mid-build

✏️
1. Sketch on Paper
Draw your screens, flows, and user journey. It doesn't have to be pretty — just clear. Paper forces you to think through the experience.
🤖
2. Show It to an LLM
Take a photo of your sketch. Upload it to ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Explain what you want to achieve. Use the AI as your thinking partner.
🔍
3. Ask for Criticism
"What am I missing?" "What could go wrong?" "Is this too complex for an MVP?" Let the AI challenge your assumptions before you invest time building.
✂️
4. Strip to Lean MVP
Remove every feature that isn't essential. Ask: "What's the ONE thing this must do well?" Ship that first, add the rest later.
Why This Matters
✓
Avoid scope creep — No surprises mid-build that derail your project
✓
Better prompts — Clear thinking = clear instructions to AI
✓
Faster iteration — Smaller scope = quicker to build & test
✓
Ship sooner — A working MVP beats a perfect idea in your head
"The goal isn't to build everything. It's to build the right thing — fast."

Build Strategy

One Thing at a Time

You can't build everything at once — here's how to focus your effort

🔀
Focus on ONE Flow
Don't try to build every feature. Pick the most important user journey and make it work end-to-end before moving to the next one.
Example: Content Tool
First flow: User enters niche → Gets topic ideas
Later: Save topics, expand to posts, scheduling
+
⚖️
Function OR Form
Decide: are you testing if the idea works (functionality) or how it looks (appearance)? Trying both at once leads to confusion.
Pick Your Priority
Functionality first: Ugly but works = validated idea
Appearance first: Beautiful mockup for feedback
🚀 Ship a working slice, not a broken whole. Iterate from there.
Ahmed Ezzat — Co-founder of Akadeemy

Your Blueprint

The PRD: Your App's Blueprint

A Product Requirements Document turns your vision into clear instructions for AI

What is a PRD?
A structured document that describes what you're building, who it's for, and how it should work — before writing any code. It's the bridge between your sketch and the AI.
1
Problem
What pain point are you solving?
2
User
Who is this for? What do they need?
3
Core Features
The essential functionality (MVP scope)
4
User Flow
Step-by-step journey through the app
5
Screens
What pages/views are needed?
6
Data
What information needs to be stored?
7
Style
Look & feel, colors, tone
🤖
Let AI Create Your PRD
"I want to build [app idea]. Help me create a PRD document that includes:

• Problem statement
• Target user persona
• MVP feature list (max 5)
• Main user flow
• Required screens
• Data to store
• Visual style direction

Keep it lean and focused on one core flow. Ask me questions if you need more clarity."
↓
