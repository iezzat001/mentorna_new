## Three Popular Apps Built with AI-Assisted Coding Tools

Here are three apps that showcase the power of AI-assisted coding platforms like Lovable and Bolt.new:

### 1. **Plinq**

**Founder:** Sabrine Matos, a growth marketer and first-time founder from Brazil.[1]

**Built with:** Lovable[1]

**Revenue & Timeline:** Plinq achieved R$2.2 million (~$456,000 USD) in annual recurring revenue after just 45 days of development to MVP. The app grew to 10,000+ users within three months with a remarkable 300% month-over-month growth rate. Following viral TikTok marketing (3 million views in one week alone), the app reached 500 million total views in just three days. Sabrine is currently raising R$1.5–2 million (~$500,000 USD) in pre-seed funding.[2][1]

**What it does:** Plinq is a women's safety app that provides instant criminal record checks by connecting to over 40 public databases, helping women access background information before trusting someone new. This addresses a critical social need, having helped women avoid 200+ potentially dangerous situations.[1]

**Why it's significant:** Despite having no formal technical coding background, Sabrine built the entire platform—website, desktop app, and backend workflows—without an engineering degree, demonstrating that AI-assisted coding democratizes product creation.[1]

***

### 2. **Puff Count**

**Founder:** Steven Cravotta, a 24-year-old self-taught app developer from Atlanta, Georgia.[3]

**Built with:** React Native with AI-assisted development.[4]

**Revenue & Timeline:** Puff Count generates $40,000 in monthly recurring revenue (~$480,000 USD annually) through a freemium model ($3.99/month, $23.99/year, or $74.99 lifetime subscriptions). Notably, this entire operation is run by a single founder with zero employees, powered primarily by viral TikTok content and word-of-mouth marketing.[4][3]

**What it does:** Puff Count is a mobile app that helps people quit vaping by tracking puffs and providing behavioral support for nicotine addiction.[4]

**Background:** Steven is a Pepperdine University student (Advertising/Marketing major) who previously developed popular apps including Wordle and Grid, showcasing his ability to identify and build for market demand.[3]

***

### 3. **PrintPigeon**

**Founder:** Yannis Karagiannidis, an experienced growth marketing professional.[5]

**Built with:** Lovable[6]

**Revenue & Timeline:** PrintPigeon launched as a revenue-generating micro-SaaS in just three days and remains profitable. While specific revenue figures are not publicly disclosed, the company demonstrates strong product-market fit and sustainable business metrics.[6]

**What it does:** PrintPigeon simplifies the process of sending letters online—users upload documents and the service handles printing and postage, saving time and money on postal tasks.[5]

**Background:** Yannis brings deep SaaS expertise from previous roles including VP of Growth at Monese (where he drove 50% year-over-year customer acquisition increases) and serves as Fractional Head of Growth for multiple AI SaaS companies. He is now serving as Founder & CEO.[5]

***

## Key Takeaway

These three apps demonstrate that AI-assisted coding tools (Lovable, Bolt.new, and AI-integrated frameworks) enable founders with diverse technical backgrounds—from non-technical growth marketers to 24-year-old developers—to launch profitable products rapidly and achieve significant revenue within months. The combined success shows that revenue-generating apps built with AI-assisted coding can scale from $456K to $480K annually while maintaining operational efficiency.
